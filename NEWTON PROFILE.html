Newton

Isaac Newton was an English mathematician, physicist, astronomer, and author who is widely recognized as one of the most influential scientists in history. He was born on January 4, 1643, in Woolsthorpe, Lincolnshire, England, and died on March 31, 1727. Newton made significant contributions to various fields of science, including mathematics, physics, and astronomy.
